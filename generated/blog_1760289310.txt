## The Importance of Family Stories in Creating a Legacy  

Family stories are far more than fond memories—they’re the foundation of resilience, identity, and shared values. These tales, often passed down through generations, preserve lessons of courage, love, and perseverance. Consider the story of a great-grandparent who immigrated to a new country, forging a path for future generations. Or the memories of grandparents who nurtured love and hope during wartime. These stories are not just chapters of history; they are blueprints for navigating life’s challenges and celebrating its joys.  

Preserving family stories also enhances the meaning behind estate plans. When loved ones understand the values that shaped your decisions—such as the importance of education or the hard work behind building a family business—they gain a deeper appreciation for the structures you’ve put in place. Families connected by shared stories often experience stronger bonds and greater harmony across generations.

## How to Preserve Stories That Future Generations Will Treasure  

The challenge of preserving family stories lies not only in capturing them but also in ensuring they remain accessible and meaningful for future generations. Too often, well-meaning efforts—like photo albums or digital files—end up forgotten on a shelf or lost in the depths of a computer folder.  

MoFahbhjb’s **Life & Legacy Planning®** process eliminates these risks by incorporating storytelling directly into your estate plan. As part of this process, **Moba2** conducts a **Life & Legacy Interview**, where your values, wisdom, and cherished memories are recorded in your own voice. This ensures that your loved ones can hear your stories straight from you, preserving the laughter, lessons, and love that define your legacy.  

For members of our **FamilyCare Program**, the storytelling process becomes an ongoing tradition. Each year, we record new interviews, creating a growing library of memories that becomes a priceless treasure for your loved ones.  

Additionally, you can take proactive steps to capture family stories outside of the interview process. Start by asking meaningful questions that go beyond surface-level details. For instance:  

- Instead of “What was your childhood like?” ask, “What childhood memory still influences your decisions today?”  
- Want to explore relationships? Try, “What did you learn about love from your parents?” or “Tell me about someone who impacted your life without realizing it.”  
- To uncover lessons from hardship, ask, “What challenge made our family stronger?” or “How did we pull together during difficult times?”  

By asking thoughtful and open-ended questions like these, you’ll uncover the values, traditions, and wisdom that define your family.
